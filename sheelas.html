Sheela-na-gigs

The Look

The Sheela-na-gig is a medieval stone carving of a naked female figure with a pose that emphasizes her genitalia. The carvings are found on churches, castles, and monasteries and are mainly located in Ireland. The original meaning of the Sheela image remains unknown. In more recent times, however, it has been used as a feminist icon, talisman, fertility figure and focus of devotional rituals.
